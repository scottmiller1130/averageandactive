The fitness industry wants you to think you need 47 different exercises, a periodized program, and a PhD in kinesiology. You don't. If you're a parent just trying to stay healthy and functional, here are the only five movements that matter.

## 1. Squat

Barbell, goblet, bodyweight ‚Äî doesn't matter. Squatting keeps your legs strong, your knees healthy, and your ability to get off the floor intact as you age. I do goblet squats with a dumbbell 2x per week. Nothing fancy.

## 2. Hinge (Deadlift)

Picking heavy stuff off the ground. This builds your back, glutes, and hamstrings ‚Äî the muscles that protect your spine when you're lifting kids, groceries, or furniture. Romanian deadlifts with dumbbells are my go-to.

## 3. Push (Bench Press / Push-Ups)

Upper body pushing. Bench press, push-ups, overhead press ‚Äî pick one or rotate. This keeps your chest, shoulders, and arms strong for carrying kids, pushing strollers, and everyday life.

## 4. Pull (Rows / Pull-Ups)

The counterbalance to all that pushing (and all that sitting at a desk). Rows and pull-ups build the back muscles that keep your posture solid. I do dumbbell rows every upper body day.

## 5. Carry (Farmer's Walk)

Grab two heavy dumbbells and walk. That's it. This builds grip strength, core stability, and total body endurance. It's also the most 'real life' exercise there is ‚Äî when was the last time you carried a sleeping child plus three bags of groceries up the stairs?

## The Bottom Line

Master these five movements and you're set. You don't need fancy equipment, a gym membership, or a complicated program. Just these five, done consistently, with gradual progress. That's the whole recipe.
